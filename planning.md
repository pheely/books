## What is RRIF tax bomb

The **RRIF "Tax Bomb"** refers to two specific scenarios where the tax-deferred savings in your RRSP (which eventually must become a RRIF) turn into a massive tax liability that can wipe out **40% to 54%** of your wealth in a single year.

Since your portfolio is **67% RRSP**, this is the most important tax concept for your retirement planning.

### 1. The "Age 72" Income Bomb (Mandatory Minimums)
By December 31 of the year you turn **71**, you must convert your RRSP into a RRIF. Starting at age **72**, the government *forces* you to withdraw a minimum percentage of the account every year, and that percentage increases as you get older.

*   **The Trap:** If you have a large RRSP (like your ~$1M projection) and you don't touch it until age 72, the mandatory withdrawal might be **more money than you actually need**.
*   **The Result:** These forced withdrawals are added to your other income (pension, CPP, OAS). This can push you into a much higher tax bracket and, even worse, trigger the **OAS Recovery Tax (Clawback)**. In 2026, if your individual income exceeds roughly **$91,000**, the government begins taking back 15 cents of every OAS dollar you receive.

### 2. The "Estate" Bomb (Deemed Disposition)
This is the "explosion" that most people fear. When the last surviving spouse passes away, the CRA treats the entire remaining RRIF balance as if it were **withdrawn as cash on a single day**.

*   **The Trap:** If you pass away with $800,000 in your RRIF, that full $800,000 is added to your income for your final tax return.
*   **The Result:** In Ontario, an income of $800,000 hits the highest marginal tax bracket (~53.5%). 
*   **The Math:** Your children might expect to inherit $800,000, but after the "Tax Bomb" goes off, the CRA takes roughly **$420,000**, leaving your heirs with only **$380,000**.

### 3. RRIF Minimum Withdrawal Table (2026 Rates)
To see how the "bomb" grows, look at how the government forces the money out:

| Age (Start of Year) | Minimum Withdrawal % |
| :--- | :--- |
| **72** | 5.40% |
| **75** | 5.82% |
| **80** | 6.82% |
| **85** | 8.51% |
| **90** | 11.92% |

> **Example:** If your RRIF is worth **$1M** at age 85, you are *forced* to take out **$85,100**. When added to your pensions, you could be paying 40%+ tax on that money whether you spend it or not.


### 4. How to "Defuse" the Bomb
Because you are retiring at **60**, you have a **12-year window** before mandatory RRIF withdrawals begin. You can use this time to strategically lower the pressure:

1.  **Early Meltdown:** Instead of leaving the RRSP alone, start withdrawing small amounts at age 60 to fill up your lower tax brackets. It is often better to pay **20% tax now** than **54% tax later**.
2.  **TFSA Shifting:** Take money out of the RRSP (taxed at a low rate) and immediately contribute it to your **TFSA**. This "re-shelters" the money so it can grow tax-free and will *never* be part of a tax bomb for your children.
3.  **Spousal RRIF Election:** When you convert to a RRIF, you can elect to use the **younger spouse’s age** to calculate minimum withdrawals. This keeps the mandatory amounts lower for longer, giving you more control.
4.  **Charitable Giving:** If you leave your RRIF to a charity in your will, the tax credit from the donation can often cancel out the tax bill from the "Deemed Disposition."
